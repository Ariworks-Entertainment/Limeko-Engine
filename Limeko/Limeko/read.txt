# Important

Limeko will always stay open source, and will never be a paid program.
My goal out of Limeko isn't money, but a fast, reliable way to make games.

Want to support? Great, just donate.
Don't? Doesn't matter, you still get the same features as everyone else.

I may be fifteen, I may be a random girl on the internet, but shareholders don't
have a say in what I make of my own creations, and they never will.


# Future & Plans

Obviously as of right now everything is in Program.cs.
Eventually, they'll be in separate classes, like "Editor.cs" or "Renderer.cs".
I'm just working on getting it functional before I make it pretty.


# Community

Post-launch, I'll make updates, I'll add features, and I'll keep it working for as long
as I can.

However, I'm one person, and I'm sure there are people who know more than me.
So, that's why the license is GNU Lesser;

Make your own modifications, add features, improve stuff, and if you think it's
ready enough to push to the real thing, send a push request--We're happy to consider.

Limeko is *not* a giant corporation-driven program. It's a community-driven tool made
as a love-letter to all of your favorite engines--Source, Source 2, Unity, Unreal, S&box--we
love it all. (Even if Unity can be finnicky, and Unreal's performance can be iffy.)


# Final Notes

While Limeko is still in development, and isn't even slightly ready for games to be made,
love is still poured into it daily, and I plan to work on it for as long as I can.

As for 'why', Limeko was going to be Ariworks' in-house engine. Then I figured, "Why not hit
two birds with one stone?" Well, here comes the stone.
